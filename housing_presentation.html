Motivation

Why Housing Markets?

“The U.S. housing market is a cornerstone of national economic health, sensitive to inflation, unemployment, mortgage conditions, interest-rate policy, supply, and market timing.”

Market Complexity

Housing prices react to several interacting macroeconomic and financial variables.

Stakeholder Need

Analysts, students, institutions, and policymakers need accessible evidence.

ML Opportunity

Machine learning and diagnostics help test predictive value and model risk.

Problem

Research Question

  • How can data mining and machine learning analyze and predict U.S. housing-market trends using macroeconomic indicators?
  • The analytical challenge is time dependence, lagged behavior, and interacting variables.
  • The communication challenge is making model evidence clear for non-technical users.
  • The product challenge is transforming the analysis into a usable Streamlit dashboard.

Forecasting As Decision Support

How to present it Forecasting is used as a scenario-support tool, not as a guaranteed future truth.

The correct defense message is: use forecasts together with evaluation metrics, OLAP period summaries, diagnostics, and paper-review theory.
Future Work

Limitations & Future Directions

  • National-level data hides regional differences.
  • Forecasting is educational, not financial advice.
  • Correlation and ML prediction do not prove causality.
  • The static dataset needs manual refresh.
  • Add regional data from city/state sources.
  • Connect live APIs such as FRED, Census, or market feeds.
  • Add SHAP explainability and stronger chatbot grounding.
  • Deploy with authentication, monitoring, and drift alerts.

Final Conclusion
